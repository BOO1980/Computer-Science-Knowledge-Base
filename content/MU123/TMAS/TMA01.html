Question 2

a: Alex is following a recipe to make a pavlova that uses 280 grams of sugar and 6 eggs. However, they drop one egg and so only have the five remaining eggs to use. Calculate the quantity of sugar that Alex should now use to make their pavlova. Give your answer in grams, correct to 2 significant figures.

Answer: The recipe states that there is 280 grams of sugar for 6 eggs. Therefore, for 1 egg there is 280/6 = 46.666... grams of sugar. For 5 eggs there is 5 x 46.666... = 233.333... grams of sugar. Rounding to 2 significant figures gives 230 grams of sugar.

b: The book Is maths real? by Eugenia Cheng has 336 pages. An online preview of the book includes 39 pages. Calculate the percentage of pages that are included in the online preview. Give your answer correct to the nearest whole number.

Answer: The book has 336 pages and the online preview has 39 pages. Therefore, the percentage of pages in the online preview is (39/336) x 100 = 11.607...%, which rounds to 12%.

c:A farm has 140 sheep.

i: 45% of these sheep are Texel sheep. Calculate the number of Texel sheep on the farm.

Answer: The farm has 140 sheep and 45% of these are Texel sheep. Therefore, the number of Texel sheep is (45/100) x 140 = 63 sheep.

ii:Of these Texel sheep, 36 are lambs (are under 1 year old). Write the number of Texel lambs as a fraction of the total number of Texel sheep on the farm. Give your answer both as an unsimplified fraction, and as a fraction in its simplest form.

Answer: There are 63 Texel sheep on the farm, of which 36 are lambs. Therefore, the number of Texel lambs as a fraction of the total number of Texel sheep is 36/63, which simplifies to (36 ÷ 9)/(63 ÷ 9) = 4/7.

Bo, Cal and Drew are each crocheting a scarf.

i:At one point, Bo’s scarf is 87 cm long and Cal’s scarf is 19% longer than Bo’s. Calculate the length of Cal’s scarf. Give your answer in centimetres correct to the nearest centimetre.

Answer: Bo’s scarf is 87 cm long, so Cal’s scarf is 19% longer than this. Therefore, the length of Cal’s scarf is 87 + (19/100) x 87 = 87 x (1 + 19/100) = 87 x (119/100) = 103.53 cm, which rounds to 104 cm.

ii: At this point, Drew’s scarf is 8/11 of the length of Cal’s scarf. Calculate the length of Drew’s scarf. Give your answer in centimetres, correct to the nearest centimetre.

Answer: Cal’s scarf is 104 cm long, so Drew’s scarf is 8/11 of this length. Therefore, the length of Drew’s scarf is (8/11) x 104 = 75.636... cm, which rounds to 76 cm.

Question 3

  • Erin completes a training ride of 28.5 kilometres in 2 hours and 45 minutes. Calculate their average speed for this ride, giving your answer in kilometres per hour (km/h) correct to one decimal place
    • To find the average speed, we need to divide the total distance by the total time.
    • First, we need to convert the time into hours. 2 hours and 45 minutes is equal to 2.75 hours (since 45 minutes is 0.75 of an hour).
    • Now we can calculate the average speed:
    • Average speed = Total distance / Total time
    • Average speed = 28.5 km / 2.75 hours = 10.3636... km/h
    • Rounding to one decimal place, the average speed is 10.4 km/h.
  • The first bridlepath (horse riding trail) on Erin’s training ride measures 34.6 cm on a map which has a scale of 1 : 16 000. Calculate the corresponding distance covered on the ground. Give your answer in kilometres, correct to two significant figures.
    • To find the corresponding distance on the ground, we can use the scale of the map.
    • The scale 1 : 16,000 means that 1 cm on the map represents 16,000 cm on the ground.
    • First, we need to convert the distance on the map from centimeters to centimeters on the ground:
    • Distance on the ground = Distance on the map x Scale factor
    • Distance on the ground = 34.6 cm x 16,000 = 553,600 cm
    • Now, we need to convert centimeters to kilometers. There are 100,000 cm in a kilometer:
    • Distance in kilometers = Distance in cm / 100,000
    • Distance in kilometers = 553,600 cm / 100,000 = 5.536 km
    • Rounding to two significant figures, the distance covered on the ground is 5.5 km.
