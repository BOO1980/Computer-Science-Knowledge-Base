Numbers

  • Numbers are used to specify quantity or amount. For example, people give their ages as a number of years.
  • Votes in elections may be described in thousands.
  • Temperatures are measured in positive and negative degrees Celsius.
  • Number smaller than one may be expressed as fractions or decimals.

    • Whole Numbers

    • Whole numbers arise from counting, for example, the number of sheep in a field or the number of votes in an election.
    • Our everyday number system is the decimal system, where the position of a digital within the number determines whether it represents units, tens, thousands or hundreds, etc.

    Using the place value.

    • Writing numbers in place value columns gives an easy way of multiplying by 10, 100, 1000 and so on.
    • For example, to multiply 345 by 10, we can write the number in place value columns and then move each digit one place to the left.
    • Similarly, to multiply by 100 we move each digit two places to the left.
    • To divide by 10, 100 or 1000 we move the digits to the right.
    • For example, to divide 345 by 10 we move each digit one place to the right.

    Number lines

    • Number lines are a visual representation of numbers in order.
    • They can be used to help understand addition and subtraction.
    • For example, to add 3 + 2, you can start at 3 on the number line and count 2 spaces to the right to get 5.
