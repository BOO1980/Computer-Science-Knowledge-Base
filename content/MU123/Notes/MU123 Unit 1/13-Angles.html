Angles

  • An angle is formed when two lines meet at a point called the vertex.
  • Angles are measured in degrees (°) using a protractor.
  • A full circle is 360°, a half circle is 180°, and a right angle is 90°.
  • Types of angles:
    • Acute angle: less than 90°
    • Right angle: exactly 90°
    • Obtuse angle: between 90° and 180°
    • Straight angle: exactly 180°
    • Reflex angle: between 180° and 360°
  • The sum of angles around a point is 360°.
  • The sum of angles on a straight line is 180°.
  • The sum of angles in a triangle is always 180°.
  • The sum of angles in a quadrilateral is always 360°.
  • Complementary angles are two angles that add up to 90°.
  • Supplementary angles are two angles that add up to 180°.

Questions

Question 1: A plan from a DIY booklet shows a shelf and its metal bracket. The shelf makes a right angle with the wall. The size of one of the angles is given (28°). What is the size of the other angle?

Answer: Since the shelf makes a right angle with the wall, the two angles must add up to 90°. Therefore, the size of the other angle can be calculated as follows:
Other angle = 90° - 28° = 62°
So, the size of the other angle is 62°.
