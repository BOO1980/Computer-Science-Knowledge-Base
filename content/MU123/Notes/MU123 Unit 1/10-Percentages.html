Percentages

  • A percentage is a way of expressing a number as a fraction of 100. The symbol for percentage is %.
  • To convert a percentage to a decimal, divide by 100. For example, 25% = 25 ÷ 100 = 0.25.
  • To convert a decimal to a percentage, multiply by 100. For example, 0.75 = 0.75 × 100 = 75%.
  • To find a percentage of a quantity, multiply the quantity by the percentage (as a decimal). For example, to find 20% of 50, calculate 50 × 0.20 = 10.
  • To increase a quantity by a percentage, multiply the quantity by (1 + percentage as a decimal). For example, to increase 100 by 15%, calculate 100 × (1 + 0.15) = 115.
  • To decrease a quantity by a percentage, multiply the quantity by (1 - percentage as a decimal). For example, to decrease 80 by 25%, calculate 80 × (1 - 0.25) = 60.
  • The word "cent" is taken from the latin word "centum," which means "hundred."

Questions

Question 1: store is offering 15% off all electrical goods during its summer sale. What would you pay for a washing machine that usually costs £310?

To find the sale price, first calculate 15% of £310:

15% of £310 = 0.15 × £310 = £46.50

Now, subtract this discount from the original price:

Sale price = £310 - £46.50 = £263.50
