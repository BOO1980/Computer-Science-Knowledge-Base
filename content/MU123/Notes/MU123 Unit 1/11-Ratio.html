Ratio

  • A ratio is a way to compare two or more quantities.
  • Ratios can be expressed in three different ways: using a colon (e.g., 3:2), as a fraction (e.g., 3/2), or in words (e.g., "3 to 2").
  • To simplify a ratio, divide both parts by their greatest common divisor (GCD). For example, the ratio 8:12 can be simplified to 2:3 by dividing both parts by 4.
  • Ratios can be scaled up or down by multiplying or dividing both parts by the same number. For example, the ratio 2:3 can be scaled up to 4:6 by multiplying both parts by 2.
  • Ratios can be used to find proportions. For example, if the ratio of boys to girls in a class is 2:3, this means that for every 2 boys, there are 3 girls.
  • To divide a quantity in a given ratio, first find the total number of parts in the ratio, then divide the quantity by this total to find the value of one part, and finally multiply by the number of parts for each section. For example, to divide £60 in the ratio 2:3, first find the total parts (2 + 3 = 5), then divide £60 by 5 to get £12 per part, and finally multiply by 2 for the first section (£12 × 2 = £24)

Questions

Question 1: A family on holiday in Florida is considering buying a souvenir which is on sale for $22. If the rate of exchange at the time is £1 = $1.42 approximately how much does the souvenir cost in pounds?

To find the cost in pounds, divide the price in dollars by the exchange rate:

Cost in pounds = $22 ÷ $1.42 ≈ £15.49
