Powers

Powers, also known as exponents, are a way to express repeated multiplication. For example, 2 raised to the power of 3 (written as 2^3) means 2 × 2 × 2, which equals 8.

In general, a number a raised to the power of n (written as a^n) is defined as:

a^n = a × a × ... × a (n times)

Here are some examples of powers:

  • 3^2 = 3 × 3 = 9
  • 5^3 = 5 × 5 × 5 = 125
  • 10^0 = 1 (any number raised to the power of 0 is 1)
  • 2^4 = 2 × 2 × 2 × 2 = 16
