Negative Numbers

  • A negative number is a number that is less than zero. It is represented with a minus sign (−) in front of the number. For example, −3 is a negative number.
  • Negative numbers are used to represent values that are below a certain reference point, such as temperatures below freezing, debts, or elevations below sea level.
  • On a number line, negative numbers are located to the left of zero. The further left you go, the smaller the value of the negative number.
  • Negative numbers can be used in mathematical operations just like positive numbers. For example, subtracting a negative number is the same as adding its positive counterpart.
  • In some contexts, negative numbers can represent opposite values. For example, in finance, a profit of $5 can be represented as +5, while a loss of $5 can be represented as -5.
  • When working with negative numbers, it is important to remember the rules of arithmetic. For example, when multiplying or dividing two negative numbers, the result is a positive number.
  • Negative numbers are commonly used in various fields, including mathematics, physics, engineering, and economics.

Negative symbol as opposite

  • The opposite of a number is the number on the other side of 0 number line, and the same distance from 0
  • -4 is the opposite of 4
  • -8 is the opposite of 8
  • 231 is the opposite of -231

Rational Numbers of the number line

  • Rational numbers are numbers that can be expressed as a fraction of two integers, where the denominator is not zero. This includes both positive and negative numbers, as well as zero itself.

Absolute Value

  • The absolute value of a number is its distance from 0 on the number line, regardless of direction.
  • Absolute values are always non-negative.
  • The absolute value of -5 is 5, and the absolute value of 5 is also 5.

Multiplication of Negative Numbers

  • When you multiply two negative numbers, the result is a positive number.
  • For example: (−2) × (−3) = 6
  • When you multiply a negative number by a positive number, the result is a negative number.
  • For example: (−2 ) × 3 = −6
  • When you multiply a positive number by a negative number, the result is also a negative number.
  • For example: 2 × (−3) = −6
  • When you multiply two positive numbers, the result is a positive number.
  • For example: 2 × 3 = 6

Division of Negative Numbers

  • When you divide two negative numbers, the result is a positive number.
  • For example: (−6) ÷ (−2) = 3
  • When you divide a negative number by a positive number, the result is a negative number.
  • For example: (−6) ÷ 2 = −3
  • When you divide a positive number by a negative number, the result is also a negative number.
  • For example: 6 ÷ (−2) = −3
  • When you divide two positive numbers, the result is a positive number.
  • For example: 6 ÷ 2 = 3
