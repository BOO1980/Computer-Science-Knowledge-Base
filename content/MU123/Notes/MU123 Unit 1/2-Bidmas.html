BIDMAS

BIDMAS is an acronym that helps remember the order of operations in mathematics:

  • B - Brackets
  • I - Indices (or powers)
  • D - Division
  • M - Multiplication
  • A - Addition
  • S - Subtraction

When solving mathematical expressions, operations should be performed in this order to ensure accurate results.

For example, in the expression 3 + 4 × 2, you would first perform the multiplication (4 × 2 = 8), and then the addition (3 + 8 = 11). If you were to perform the addition first, you would incorrectly calculate it as (3 + 4) × 2 = 14.

Understanding BIDMAS is crucial for solving complex mathematical problems and ensuring clarity in calculations.

Exercises

Example 1:

  1. 8 - 2 + 5 - 1
    • (addition and subtraction from left to right)
    • Step 1: 8 - 2 = 6
    • Step 2: 6 + 5 = 11
    • Step 3: 11 - 1 = 10
  2. 5 + 12 / 4
    • (division before addition)
    • Step 1: 12 / 4 = 3
    • Step 2: 5 + 3 = 8
  3. 4 x 52
    • (exponentiation before multiplication)
    • Step 1: 52 = 25
    • Step 2: 4 x 25 = 100
      • (5-3)x4
