Radius

  • The radius of a circle is the distance from the center of the circle to any point on its circumference.
  • The radius is half the length of the diameter, which is the distance across the circle through its center. Therefore, if you know the diameter (d) of a circle, you can find the radius (r) using the formula: r = d / 2.
  • The radius is an important measurement in geometry and is used in various calculations involving circles, such as finding the area and circumference.
  • The area (A) of a circle can be calculated using the formula: A = πr², where r is the radius and π (pi) is approximately 3.14159.
  • The circumference (C) of a circle can be calculated using the formula: C = 2πr, where r is the radius.
  • In three-dimensional geometry, the radius is also used to describe spheres. The radius of a sphere is the distance from its center to any point on its surface.

Questions

Question 1: A garden is 7m x 5m and has a circular flower bed in the center. with a radius of 1.5m. . If 400 grams of grass seed covers 8 square metres, how much grass seed will be needed to re-seed the lawn?

First, calculate the area of the garden:

Area of garden = length × width = 7m × 5m = 35m²

Next, calculate the area of the circular flower bed using the formula A = πr²:

Area of flower bed = π × (1.5m)² = π × 2.25m² ≈ 7.07m²

Now, subtract the area of the flower bed from the area of the garden to find the area of the lawn:

Area of lawn = Area of garden - Area of flower bed = 35m² - 7.07m² ≈ 27.93m²

Finally, calculate the amount of grass seed needed:

Grass seed needed = (Area of lawn / Area covered by 400 grams) × 400 grams = (27.93m² / 8m²) × 400 grams ≈ 1396.5 grams

So, approximately 1396.5 grams of grass seed will be needed to re-seed the lawn.
