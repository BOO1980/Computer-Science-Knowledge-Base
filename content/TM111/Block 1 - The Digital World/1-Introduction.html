Introduction

  • You will experience and investigate a wide range of aspects of the digital world
  • Many things we enjoy, such as music, images, emails and social networking are based on digital technology.
  • You will see how advances in these technologies have led to them playing a significant role in our lives.
  • You will explore some of the issues and debates that surround the use of digital technologies in a range of everyday situations
  • Block 1 introduces some key skills for study, including communication, numeracy, practical and professional skills and digital and information literacy (DIL) skills to help you become and effective learner
  • Part 1: Living in a digital world explores what is meant by the digital world in terms of the techninolgies involved
  • it introduces some of the legal and ethical issues surrounding the use of digital technologies.
  • Part 2: The evolving computer: discusses how computers are ubiquitous in that they are everywhere around us
  • Binary numbers to code digital representations are also introduced
  • Part 3: Digital media: looks at how images and sound are represented in binary numbers in computing
  • You will use a sound editing program called Audacity to record and manipulate sound
  • Part 4: A World built of data: explores the relationship between data, computer and humans
  • Part 5: Weaving the web: explains how the web works and includes building simple web pages using HTML codes and editing tools
  • Part 6: Crossing boundaries: looks at human-computer interaction (HCI) as the shared boundaries between the user and the computer
  • You will be introduced to concepts such as usability and accessibility
  • You will start your personal and professional development planning
