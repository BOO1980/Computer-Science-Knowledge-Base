Block 1: The Digital World

Part 1: Living in a Digital World

  • Digital Technologies: Tools and systems that generate, store, and process data.
  • Digital World: The environment created by digital technologies and their impact on society.
  • Information Society: A society where the creation, distribution, and manipulation of information is a significant economic, political, and cultural activity.
  • Internet: A global network of interconnected computers and servers that communicate using standardized protocols.
  • Internetwork: A collection of interconnected networks that communicate with each other using standardized protocols.
  • Networks: Systems of interconnected computers and devices that communicate with each other.
  • Ubiquity: The state of being everywhere at once, often used to describe the pervasive nature of digital technologies.
  • Smart Devices: Objects that incorporate computer technologies and can communicate with other devices over the internet.
  • Subscriber Trunk Dialling (STD): A system introduced in the UK in 1959 that allowed users to dial long-distance calls without going through an operator.
  • Ubiquitous Computing: The integration of computing into everyday objects and environments, making technology seamlessly available.
  • World Wide Web: A system of interlinked hypertext documents accessed via the internet, allowing users to view and interact with content.
  • Convergence: The process by which previously distinct technologies and industries become more closely integrated and interdependent.
