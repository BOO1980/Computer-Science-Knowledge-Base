Keywords

This page contains keywords related to the TM111 module.

Block 1: The Digital World

Part 1: Living in a Digital World

  • Digital Technologies: Tools and systems that generate, store, and process data.
  • Digital World: The environment created by digital technologies and their impact on society.
  • Information Society: A society where the creation, distribution, and manipulation of information is a significant economic, political, and cultural activity.
  • Internet: A global network of interconnected computers and servers that communicate using standardized protocols.
  • Internetwork: A collection of interconnected networks that communicate with each other using standardized protocols.
  • Networks: Systems of interconnected computers and devices that communicate with each other.
  • Ubiquity: The state of being everywhere at once, often used to describe the pervasive nature of digital technologies.
  • Smart Devices: Objects that incorporate computer technologies and can communicate with other devices over the internet.
  • Subscriber Trunk Dialling (STD): A system introduced in the UK in 1959 that allowed users to dial long-distance calls without going through an operator.
  • Ubiquitous Computing: The integration of computing into everyday objects and environments, making technology seamlessly available.
  • World Wide Web: A system of interlinked hypertext documents accessed via the internet, allowing users to view and interact with content.
  • Convergence: The process by which previously distinct technologies and industries become more closely integrated and interdependent.
  • Generative AI: A type of artificial intelligence that can create new content based on the data it has been trained on.
  • Prompt: A text input given to a generative AI model to elicit a specific response or generate content.
  • Chatbots: AI systems that can engage in conversation with users, often used for customer support or information retrieval.
  • Chat GPT-3: A state-of-the-art language processing AI model developed by OpenAI, capable of generating human-like text based on given prompts.
  • DALL-E2: An AI model developed by OpenAI that generates images from textual descriptions.
  • Large Language Models (LLMs): A type of generative AI model specifically designed to understand and generate human-like text.
  • Information Society: A society where the creation, distribution, and manipulation of information is a significant economic, political, and cultural activity.
  • Network Society: A social structure made up of networks powered by digital communication technologies.
  • Knowledge Society: A society where knowledge is created, shared, and utilized to drive economic and social development.
  • Digital Divide: The gap between individuals who have access to digital technologies and those who do not.
  • SMS (Short Message Service): A text messaging service that allows users to send and receive short text messages on mobile devices.
  • Instant Messaging (IM): A real-time text-based communication service that allows users to exchange messages over the internet.
  • ATMs (Automated Teller Machines): Electronic banking outlets that allow customers to complete basic transactions without the need for a human teller.
  • Database: An organized collection of data that can be easily accessed, managed, and updated.
